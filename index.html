Systematic Factor Portfolio

A quantitative equity and fixed-income strategy built for institutional capital protection.

This document presents the full technical architecture of a systematic factor-based portfolio managing CAD 100M across North American equities and bonds. The system combines six academic risk premia, an adaptive regime detection engine, and a crash-protection overlay to deliver consistent returns while limiting drawdowns. Built by Laurie Fiset as part of GSF-3120 (Quantitative Finance Seminar) at Université Laval, Winter 2026.

02 Factor Model — Six Academic Premia

Momentum 24% Price Data

Cross-sectional momentum following Jegadeesh & Titman (1993). Returns over the past 12 months, skipping the most recent month to avoid short-term reversal.

RISK_ON: Score = Return(t-252 to t-21) → percentile rank
RISK_OFF: Score = Price / 52-week High → percentile rank
// In RISK_OFF, switches to 52-week high ratio (George & Hwang 2004)
// Avoids momentum crashes during market reversals
Jegadeesh & Titman (1993), Journal of Finance: Stocks with strong past returns continue to outperform over 3-12 month horizons. Documented across 200+ years of global data.
Value 35% Compustat

Earnings Yield (Net Income TTM / Market Capitalization). A modernized value definition that avoids intangible asset biases inherent in traditional book-to-market ratios.

Earnings Yield = Net Income (TTM) / Market Cap → percentile rank
// Quarterly data from Compustat with 45-day publication lag
Fama & French (1992/2015): The value premium is documented over 60+ years. Negative correlation with momentum (~-0.3), providing natural diversification within the composite signal.
Profitability (RMW) 15% Compustat

Gross Profit over Total Assets, following Novy-Marx (2013). "Robust Minus Weak" in Fama-French 5-factor terminology.

GPOA = Gross Profit / Total Assets → percentile rank
Novy-Marx (2013), JFE: Gross profitability predicts cross-sectional returns as reliably as book-to-market. The only FF5 factor that has consistently delivered a premium across all tested periods.
PEAD 10% Compustat

Post-Earnings Announcement Drift. Standardized Unexpected Earnings (SUE) measure capturing the market's under-reaction to earnings surprises.

SUE = (EPS_actual - EPS_expected) / std(surprises) → percentile rank
// Distinct from momentum, not subsumed by FF5 factors
Bernard & Thomas (1989), Journal of Accounting Research: After a positive earnings surprise, prices continue drifting upward for 60-90 days. One of the most robust anomalies in finance.
Investment (CMA) 8% Compustat

"Conservative Minus Aggressive" from Fama & French (2015). Firms that invest conservatively tend to outperform aggressive investors.

Score = Inverse(YoY Total Asset Growth) → percentile rank
Low Volatility 8% Price Data

Inverse of 12-month realized volatility. Low-volatility stocks deliver higher risk-adjusted returns than the CAPM predicts.

Score = 1 / (Annualized Vol + 0.01) → percentile rank
van Vliet (CFA Institute 2024): 2.1% alpha after controlling for all other factors. The low-volatility anomaly remains one of the strongest challenges to efficient market theory.
Composite Score — Dynamic Regime Weighting
Score_i = Sum(weight_j x percentile_rank_j(stock_i))

RISK_ON: 0.28 Mom + 0.35 Val + 0.15 Prof + 0.02 LowVol + 0.08 Inv + 0.12 PEAD
RISK_OFF: 0.12 Mom + 0.30 Val + 0.15 Prof + 0.15 LowVol + 0.08 Inv + 0.20 PEAD

In RISK_OFF, the model shifts weight toward defensive factors (Low Volatility, PEAD) and away from momentum. Factor weights are calibrated from academic literature, not optimized in-sample.

>
Anti-data-mining discipline: A grid search over 45,801 weight combinations suggested PEAD at 37% and Value at 1%. This was deliberately not followed. Harvey, Liu & Zhu (2016) show that the majority of published factors are false positives. All 15 factor pairs maintain correlations below 0.37.

04 Crash-Only Overlay — Three Layers of Protection

Layered Risk Management Architecture

During market stress, three mechanisms activate sequentially, each adding a layer of downside protection:

LayerMechanismTriggerEffect
1Regime Weight AdjustmentRegime switches to RISK_OFFMomentum weight: 28% → 12%
Low Vol weight: 2% → 15%
2Risk-Managed MomentumRegime = RISK_OFFMomentum calculation switches from
12m return to 52-week high ratio
3Crash-Only OverlayRISK_OFF + 2+ stress flags
+ confidence ≥ 70%		Equity multiplier ×0.70
Equities: 58.5% → ~41%
Overlay Mechanics
ENTRY: RISK_OFF confirmed + stress score ≥ 2 of 4 flags + confidence ≥ 70%
  Flags: VIX > 30 | SMA20 < SMA50 or DD_20d ≤ -8% | High realized vol | Wide credit spreads

EFFECT: exposure_multiplier = 1.0 - (confidence x 0.4), floor = 0.70
  Equities: 58.5% x 0.70 = ~41%, surplus reallocated to bonds

EXIT: Stress score ≤ 1 flag + 10-day cooldown to prevent whipsaw
>
COVID-19 case study: The overlay activated in late February 2020, reducing equity exposure from 58.5% to approximately 41%. Combined with the RMOM switch to the 52-week high signal, the portfolio drew down only -8.7% while the S&P 500 fell -34%. This three-layer system is the primary driver of the strategy's crisis resilience.
Risk-Managed Momentum (RMOM)

Based on Barroso & Santa-Clara (2015, Review of Financial Studies). Classical momentum is highly profitable but subject to devastating crashes during market reversals. RMOM addresses this by switching the momentum signal in RISK_OFF from the 12-month return to the 52-week high ratio, which does not exhibit the same crash behavior.

05 Portfolio Optimization

Ledoit-Wolf Shrinkage Covariance
Sigma_shrunk = (1 - alpha) x Sigma_sample + alpha x Sigma_target
// alpha = analytically optimal shrinkage intensity
// Sigma_target = diagonal matrix (individual variances)
// Lookback = 252 days, annualized

The sample covariance matrix is highly noisy with 753 securities and 252 observations. Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage (2004) regularizes the estimate by pulling it toward a stable diagonal target, producing more robust portfolio weights.

Adaptive Smoothing
w_final = (1 - lambda) x w_previous + lambda x w_optimal

Normal regime: lambda = 0.40 (gradual adjustment, reduces turnover)
Regime change: lambda = 0.85 (fast reaction to new market conditions)
Degenerate state: lambda = 1.00 (full reset if previous portfolio > 50% cash)

The OOS period encompasses COVID-19, the fastest rate-hiking cycle in 40 years, the SVB banking crisis, and the 2025 tariff shock. The regime detector was active 36.6% of the time in RISK_OFF (vs. 17.6% in-sample), confirming its adaptive response to elevated market stress.

What "frozen hyperparameters" means: All factor weights, regime thresholds, overlay rules, allocation splits, smoothing parameters, and optimization constraints were set using 2012-2019 data only. The OOS test runs the exact same model on data it has never seen. Adaptive components (covariance estimation, factor scores, regime state) update naturally with new market data — as they would in a live deployment.

08 Identified Improvements & Next Steps

Execution & Trading Infrastructure

Market impact model (Almgren-Chriss): Estimate execution costs as a function of order size, ADV, and volatility using TAQ or Bloomberg data. Produces realistic net returns.

VWAP/TWAP execution algorithms: Slice block orders according to historical intraday volume profiles to minimize market impact. Directly relevant to institutional portfolio trading.

Transaction Cost Analysis (TCA): Post-trade reporting module measuring implementation shortfall, timing cost, and opportunity cost per rebalancement.

Bond Factor Model v2 (Bloomberg)

Carry (30%): YTM via Bloomberg YAS_YLD_SPREAD. Expected impact: +30–50 bps on bond sleeve.

Rate Momentum (20%): 3m/12m sovereign rate changes via USGG10YR. Replaces static REGIME_BOND_MIX with dynamic duration timing.

Spread Momentum (25%): OAS variation via OAS_SPREAD_BID. Avoids credit before corrections. +20–40 bps.

Rolldown (10%) + Duration Timing (15%): Yield curve shape analysis for tactical duration positioning. +30–50 bps combined.

Short-Term Improvements

Composite benchmark: Add a 60/40 blended benchmark (40% SPY + 20% XIU.TO + 25% AGG + 15% XBB.TO) to isolate factor alpha from asset allocation effects. The current S&P 500 benchmark overstates crisis protection.

Variable risk-free rate: Replacing the fixed 5% Rf with the DTB3 series from FRED would better reflect the actual rate environment (~10 line change).

Dynamic liquidity constraints: Replace static $1M/day floor with ADV-based constraints (max 10–15% of 20-day ADV per position) using Bloomberg or Yahoo Finance volume data.

HMM/VIX redundancy analysis: Computing the Spearman correlation between the HMM and VIX signals would quantify potential redundancy and inform better weight calibration.

Strategic Enhancements

Survivorship bias correction: Integrate CRSP delisted tickers via WRDS to reconstruct the historical S&P 500 universe at each rebalancing date.

Alternative safe havens: Adding gold or commodity ETFs (GLD, IAU, DJP) as defensive assets would address the 2022 limitation where bonds and equities fell simultaneously.

Granular ESG integration: Replacing the current sector-level proxy with security-level Sustainalytics ratings via Bloomberg would provide meaningful ESG differentiation.

Long-short extension: If the mandate permits, adding a short book would approximately double the strategy’s Sharpe ratio, based on academic benchmarks for factor strategies.

Known Limitations

The following limitations are documented and quantified, not discovered post-hoc:

Positive equity-bond correlation (2022): When both asset classes fall together, the defensive rotation toward bonds becomes counterproductive. The portfolio lost -12.1% vs. -8.0% for a static allocation during this period. This is a structural limitation of any risk-on/risk-off framework.

Benchmark selection: The current S&P 500 benchmark does not reflect the portfolio’s 25–40% bond allocation, overstating crisis alpha and understating bull-market performance.

Survivorship bias: The universe includes 21 historical tickers, but hundreds of delisted securities are missing from the S&P 500 backtest sample.

ESG integration: The current sector-level ESG tilt provides limited differentiation within sectors. Security-level Sustainalytics scores would be more meaningful.

Factor decay risk: McLean & Pontiff (2016) document a 26% average decline in factor premia after academic publication. Diversification across six low-correlation factors mitigates but does not eliminate this risk.
